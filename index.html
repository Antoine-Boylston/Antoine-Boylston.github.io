Professional Self-Assessment

Throughout my journey in the Computer Science program at Southern New Hampshire University, I have developed a strong technical foundation while also refining my ability to think critically, communicate effectively, and solve complex problems. My background as a Controls Technician has played a significant role in shaping my approach to software development, allowing me to apply real-world troubleshooting and system-level thinking to the design and implementation of software solutions.

One of the most valuable skills I have developed is the ability to design and evaluate computing solutions using sound software engineering principles. Early in my program, my focus was primarily on writing code that functioned correctly. Over time, that perspective evolved into a deeper understanding of system design, modular architecture, and maintainability. This growth is reflected in my work, where I transitioned from simple procedural scripts to structured, scalable applications with clearly defined responsibilities.

My experience in data structures and algorithms has strengthened my ability to approach problems analytically and select appropriate solutions based on efficiency and scalability. By applying concepts such as graph representations and breadth-first search, I was able to enhance existing applications with intelligent features that provide meaningful value to users. This process reinforced the importance of choosing the right data structures to support advanced functionality.

In addition to software design and algorithms, I have developed practical experience in database and cloud-based systems. Through my work integrating AWS DynamoDB, Lambda, and API Gateway, I gained hands-on experience building a serverless architecture that connects frontend applications to scalable backend services. This experience has strengthened my understanding of modern development practices and prepared me to work with cloud technologies in a professional environment.

Communication and collaboration have also been essential components of my development. Whether through documenting code, writing technical narratives, or explaining design decisions, I have learned the importance of presenting technical information in a clear and professional manner. My experience working in team-based environments has reinforced the value of collaboration, accountability, and effective communication when delivering solutions that meet stakeholder needs.

Security and reliability have become an increasingly important part of my approach to development. Through this program, I have learned to consider potential failure points, validate inputs, and design systems that minimize risk and improve overall stability. These considerations are reflected in my work through improved input handling, structured control flow, and preparation for secure API integration.

The artifacts presented in this portfolio represent my growth across key areas of computer science. The software engineering artifact demonstrates my ability to refactor and redesign applications for maintainability and scalability. The algorithms and data structures artifact highlights my ability to apply graph-based problem solving and traversal algorithms to enhance functionality. The database artifact showcases my ability to design and implement a full-stack, serverless system using modern cloud technologies. Together, these artifacts provide a comprehensive view of my technical abilities and my progression as a developer.

As I move forward in my career, my goal is to become a Cloud Support Engineer, where I can combine my background in automation and controls with my expertise in software development and cloud computing. I am particularly interested in roles that allow me to troubleshoot complex systems, work with scalable infrastructure, and continue developing solutions that deliver real-world value. This portfolio reflects not only the skills I have developed, but also my commitment to continuous learning and professional growth.
